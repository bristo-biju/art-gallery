Composition VII

Wassily Kandinsky — 1913

Composition VII is an explosive example of Kandinsky's movement toward pure abstraction—layered color, form, and rhythmic brushwork create an orchestral visual composition rather than a single subject.

Page accents use vivid color and rhythmic layout to reflect Kandinsky's musical metaphors.
