Isle of the Dead

Arnold Böcklin — 1880 (version II)

Böcklin's mysterious masterpiece depicts a solitary boat approaching a rocky, cypress-studded island. The painting's haunting mood and ambiguous symbolism have inspired writers, composers, and filmmakers for over a century.

Movement & influence: Symbolism. The work explores themes of death, the afterlife, and the unknown, and was highly influential in late 19th-century art and culture.
