Impression, Sunrise

Claude Monet — 1872

Impression, Sunrise is the painting that lent its name to Impressionism. Monet captured shifting light and atmosphere with loose brushwork, favoring perception of a moment rather than photographic detail.

Movement & influence: This 1872 work helped coin the term "Impressionism" and encouraged plein-air painting, rapid brushwork, and a focus on light's transient effects. It influenced generations to explore perception and color over idealized form.

Page design: The page uses gentle, airy tones and soft gradients to echo Impressionism's focus on light and atmosphere; the image is presented large and centered with light padding to suggest an open plein-air space.
