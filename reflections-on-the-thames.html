The Persistence of Memory

Salvador Dalí — 1931

Dalí's iconic image of melting clocks blends hyper-realistic detail with dreamlike distortions. The work explores subconscious time, memory, and the instability of reality—core Surrealist concerns.

Movement & influence: Surrealism sought to access the unconscious through unexpected imagery and symbolic forms. Dalí's technical precision combined with bizarre subject matter made his work visually memorable and widely influential in film, literature, and design.
