Les Demoiselles d'Avignon

Pablo Picasso — 1907

Les Demoiselles d'Avignon marks a turning point toward Cubism, fragmenting space and figures into faceted planes. Picasso drew on Iberian and African art to challenge Western conventions of beauty and perspective.

Movement & influence: Cubism emphasized multiple viewpoints and geometric simplification; it reshaped 20th-century art, influencing later abstraction and design.

Visual accents on this page use strong, geometric contrasts and a subdued palette to reflect Cubism's structural focus.
